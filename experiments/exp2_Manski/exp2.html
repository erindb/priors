Instructions

This experiment consists of two blocks. The second block is self-explanatory. The first block is a bit more abstract and this is why we want to talk you through the task once. We will ask you for your estimate of different everyday quantities. For example:

Sue is a woman from the US. How many pairs of shoes do you think Sue owns?

First, we will ask you for your best guess and the minimal and maximal quantity you think is possible. Second, we are interested in your guess of the percent chance that the quantity is of a certain value or less. You will answer the question by adjusting a slider on a scale. The question we ask you and a possible answer look like this:

How likely do you think it is that Sue owns 5 pairs of shoes or less?

You will see this question up to 6 times per item. Please note that the quantities change. Because your answers are percent chances, they have to increase as the quantities increase. This is why the left boundary of the slider changes according to your rating.

How likely do you think it is that Sue owns 8 pairs of shoes or less?
